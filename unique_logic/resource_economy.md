# Resource Economy & Progression

## Resource Availability by Ring

### Ring 0 (Starter)
Available: dirt, stone, cobble, coal, iron, copper, wood (oak, birch), sand, gravel, clay
NOT available: gold, diamond, emerald, lapis, redstone, nether wart, blaze rods, obsidian (no lava)
Strategy: build basic tools, farm food, prepare for ring 1 exploration

### Ring 1 (Early)
NEW: lapis, gold (rare), spruce/dark oak wood, sweet berries, foxes (leather from wolves)
Still missing: diamond, emerald, redstone, nether materials
Strategy: upgrade to iron gear, start enchanting (lapis available), build toward ring 2

### Ring 2 (Mid)
NEW: diamond (very rare), redstone, jungle/acacia wood, all biome resources
Still missing: reliable diamond supply, nether materials, emerald
Strategy: first diamonds for enchanting table, seek jungle for bamboo/cocoa, find desert temple loot

### Ring 3+ (Late)
NEW: volcanic islands (blaze rods, nether wart, magma cream), emerald (mountain biome)
All resources now available somewhere
Strategy: establish brewing, build nether-wart farm from found wart, enchant diamond gear

### Ring 5+ (Endgame start)
Abundant ores, rare precious blocks begin appearing
Strategy: deep-ring expeditions for ore-rich islands, bank resources in ender chests

## Key Resource Bottlenecks

### Wood
- Renewable: plant saplings, grow trees on any island with dirt + light
- Essential for tools, building, crafting components
- Available from ring 0
- No bottleneck: always plantable

### Iron
- Available ring 0, becomes common ring 1+
- Critical for: buckets, anvils, shields, minecarts (future), rails
- Renewable via iron golem farms (if player builds village)
- Ore regeneration: very slow natural regen (1 ore/hour on mined islands)

### Diamond
- First available ring 2 (very rare: 1-2 per island in deepest blocks)
- Common by ring 5 (3-8 per island)
- Critical for: enchanting table, best tools/armor, beacon base
- NEVER regenerates (mine it, it's gone)
- High-ring expeditions are primary diamond source

### Blaze Rods
- ONLY from volcanic islands (ring 3+)
- Required for: brewing stand, blaze powder (brewing fuel), eyes of ender (no use here)
- Volcanic islands are dangerous (fire mobs, lava, magma blocks)
- No renewable source: must find and raid volcanic islands
- Key progression gate: no potions without blaze rods

### Nether Wart
- ONLY from volcanic island loot chests or soul sand patches (ring 3+)
- Required for: awkward potion base (all useful potions)
- Growable on soul sand once obtained: renewable after first find
- Extremely valuable: protect your nether wart farm

### Emerald
- Mountain biome islands only (ring 2+, rare ore)
- Also from villager trading
- Used for: trading, beacon base blocks
- Villager trading is renewable source
- At ring 50+: emerald blocks found on islands

### Obsidian
- Created by water + lava source interaction (standard MC)
- Lava available from ring 1+ (rare lava pools)
- Lava buckets findable in loot chests
- Required for: enchanting table, nether portal (no use here), very strong building material
- Obsidian bridges never decay (most durable bridge material)

## Renewable Resource Loops

### Food Loop
- Wheat farm: seeds from grass -> farmland + water -> wheat -> bread
- Animal farm: breed cows/pigs/chickens -> infinite meat
- Fishing: infinite fish from island water pools
- Sugar cane: plant near water -> paper + sugar
- Established by ring 0, never a problem after

### Wood Loop
- Plant saplings on dirt with space above
- Bone meal from skeletons accelerates growth
- All wood types available by ring 2 (biome-specific saplings)

### Iron/Gold Loop (Village)
- Build village: bring 2+ villagers (cure zombie villagers or find village)
- Iron golem spawns: kill for iron (3-5 ingots per golem)
- Trade with villagers for emeralds -> buy rare items
- Village-based economy is strong mid-game strategy

### Potion Loop (Ring 3+)
- Blaze rods -> blaze powder (brewing fuel)
- Nether wart farm on soul sand -> awkward potions
- Spider eyes, magma cream, ghast tears from mob farming
- Once established: infinite potions

## Item Sinks (What Consumes Resources)

### Bridge Building
- Largest resource sink in the game
- A 100-block bridge at 3-wide = 300 cobblestone minimum
- Enclosed bridge (tunnel): ~500 blocks per 100-block span
- At ring 20+: obsidian bridges needed = extremely expensive
- Long-distance expeditions can consume thousands of blocks

### Tool Durability
- Tools break, need replacement
- Diamond pick: 1561 uses (good but finite)
- High-ring mining: fast tool consumption due to dense ores
- Mending enchant critical for tool longevity

### Death
- Items lost in void are gone forever
- High-ring deaths with full inventory: catastrophic resource loss
- Armor breaks from mob combat faster at high rings (stronger mobs)
- Encourages conservative play: don't bring what you can't afford to lose

### Island Anchor Crafting
- 4 obsidian + 4 iron blocks (36 iron ingots) + 1 diamond per anchor
- Need one per outpost island
- Heavy investment for infrastructure

### Grappling Hooks
- 64 durability, consumed relatively fast
- Essential at high rings, expensive to mass-produce
- 3 iron + 2 string + 1 tripwire hook per hook

## Trading Economy (Multiplayer)

### Player-to-Player Trade
- No formal trading system (V1): just drop items on ground near each other
- Trust-based economy
- Common trades:
  - Diamonds for blaze rods (high ring explorer -> volcanic island specialist)
  - Building materials for rare loot
  - Enchanted books for raw materials
  - Bridge-building labor for resources

### Villager Trading
- Standard MC villager trading
- Librarians are most valuable: enchanted books for emeralds
- Toolsmiths/Weaponsmiths: diamond tools at Master level
- Clerics: ender pearls, glowstone, redstone
- Building a trading hall on a safe island = massive long-term advantage
